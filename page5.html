End Stage Heart Failure

Compassionate care for patients with advanced heart failure

What is End Stage Heart Failure?

End stage heart failure, also known as advanced or Stage D heart failure, is the final stage of heart failure where patients experience severe symptoms despite optimal medical therapy.

These patients have marked limitations in their ability to perform daily activities and often have frequent hospitalizations despite guideline-directed medical therapy.

Recognition and appropriate management of end stage heart failure is essential for providing compassionate, patient-centered care

Recognizing End Stage Heart Failure

Signs that a patient may be entering the end stage of heart failure:

Clinical Status

Persistent severe symptoms (NYHA Class IV) despite optimal medical therapy, with marked limitation of physical activity

Hospital Admissions

Recurrent hospitalizations or ED visits for heart failure exacerbations (≥2 in past year)

Cardiac Function

Progressive decline in cardiac function with severely reduced ejection fraction or worsening right heart failure

Refractory Symptoms

Symptoms that cannot be adequately controlled with diuretics and other medications

Organ Dysfunction

Progressive renal dysfunction, cardiac cachexia, or other end-organ damage

Quality of Life

Severely impaired quality of life with inability to perform activities of daily living

Management Principles

Patient-Centered Care

Focus on patient's goals, preferences, and quality of life. Have honest conversations about prognosis and wishes.

Symptom Management

Prioritize symptom relief - breathlessness, pain, anxiety, and other distressing symptoms using appropriate medications.

Advance Care Planning

Early discussions about advance care planning, including resuscitation status, preferred place of care, and end-of-life wishes.

Palliative Care Integration

Early involvement of palliative care services to provide holistic support for patients and families.

Medication Review

Regular review of medications - consider discontinuing therapies that no longer provide benefit or cause burden.

Family Support

Provide emotional and practical support to family members and caregivers throughout the patient's journey.

Core Principles for End Stage Heart Failure Care

  • Honest Communication: Have early, honest conversations about prognosis and what to expect, while maintaining hope and focusing on quality of life
  • Comfort Focus: Shift focus from disease-modifying treatments to comfort and quality of life when appropriate
  • Diuretic Management: Continue diuretics for symptom control but consider route (oral vs IV) and setting based on patient preference
  • Device Considerations: Discuss deactivation of ICDs if appropriate to prevent distressing shocks at end of life
  • Holistic Support: Address physical, emotional, social, and spiritual needs of patient and family
  • Coordination: Ensure seamless coordination between primary care, cardiology, palliative care, and community services
  • Preferred Place of Care: Support patient's wishes regarding place of care and death when possible

Important Considerations

  • Early Palliative Care Referral: Don't delay - palliative care can be provided alongside active treatment and improves quality of life
  • Regular Review: Reassess goals of care regularly as patient's condition changes
  • Multidisciplinary Approach: Involve heart failure team, palliative care, GP, community nurses as appropriate
  • Caregiver Burden: Recognize and address caregiver stress and provide respite when needed
  • Symptom Anticipation: Anticipate and plan for management of breathlessness, pain, anxiety, and other symptoms
