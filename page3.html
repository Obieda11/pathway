Treatment Pathways

Evidence-based pharmacological management for all HF phenotypes

Important: All treatment must take into account frailty, palliative and end of life considerations and the patient's wishes. Recent advances in pharmacological treatment have expanded options for patients, aligned with ESC guidelines.

Treatment Pathway for HFrEF (EF ≤ 40%)

Reduced Ejection Fraction Heart Failure

The Four Pillars of HFrEF Treatment

There is no set order for introduction of these medicines. However, aim to introduce all four classes early after diagnosis.

Pillar 1

ACEi / ARB / ARNI

  • ACE inhibitor (first line)
  • ARB only if ACEi intolerant
  • ARNI if recommended by HF team/cardiology (only licensed for SYMPTOMATIC HFrEF)

Titrate to maximum tolerated dose

Pillar 2

Beta Blockers

  • Bisoprolol
  • Carvedilol
  • Nebivolol

Licensed for heart failure - titrate to maximum tolerated dose

Pillar 3

MRA

  • Spironolactone
  • Eplerenone

Mineralocorticoid Receptor Antagonist

Pillar 4

SGLT2 Inhibitors

  • Dapagliflozin
  • Empagliflozin

Proven mortality and morbidity benefit

Diuretics (Symptomatic Treatment)

Loop diuretics if fluid retention - use dynamic dosing (up or down) based on clinical status

Medication Titration

  • ACE/ARB/ARNI should be titrated to maximum tolerated dose. Note: Entresto (ARNI) is only licensed for patients with HFrEF with SYMPTOMATIC heart failure.
  • Beta blockers should be titrated to maximum tolerated dose.
  • Optimisation of medicines may be managed by Primary Care with support from HF Specialist services for advice and guidance if required.
  • Seek advice and guidance from HF Specialist services to make urgent referral if complex, unstable, or at risk of hospital admission.

Safety Monitoring

Remember to check U&Es 1-2 weeks after initiation or dose titration of ACE, ARB, ARNI or MRA.

Further Specialist Management

If patient still symptomatic despite OPTIMISED ACE/ARB/ARNI, BB, MRA, SGLT2i, seek further advice from HF Specialist team as other specialist treatments may be indicated.

This may include:

  • Additional/alternative medical therapy
  • Device therapy (CRT-P, CRT-D or ICD)

Follow-up in Primary Care

  • Undertake 6-monthly review as per NICE guidance
  • Adjust diuretics as per clinical status
  • Ensure medicines titrated to maximum tolerated doses
  • Involve palliative care as required

Treatment Pathway for HFmrEF (EF 41-49%)

Mildly Reduced Ejection Fraction Heart Failure

Consider as extension of HFrEF - treat co-morbidities, add SGLT2i if diabetic/CKD

Treatment approach is similar to HFrEF with focus on the four pillars of therapy. Refer to HF Specialist services for guidance on optimal management strategy.

Treatment Pathway for HFpEF (EF ≥ 50%)

Preserved Ejection Fraction Heart Failure

Management of HFpEF focuses on symptom control with diuretics and comprehensive co-morbidity management to optimize patient outcomes.

Key Management Strategies

Heart Rate Control

Aim for a target heart rate of 70 bpm

Blood Pressure Control

Manage BP in line with Hypertension guidelines

Diabetes Control

Ensure diabetes is well controlled

Consider SGLT2 inhibitors (Dapagliflozin or Empagliflozin) if diabetic or CKD

Anaemia Management

Ensure anaemia is managed appropriately

Include iron studies in assessment

Loop Diuretic Management

Titrate loop diuretic according to symptoms

Use dynamic dosing (up or down) based on clinical status and fluid retention

Additional Considerations

  • Lifestyle modifications: Weight management, exercise programs, dietary sodium restriction
  • Regular monitoring: 6-monthly review as per NICE guidance
  • Specialist referral: If symptoms inadequately controlled or diagnostic uncertainty

Key Treatment Principles

  • HFrEF: Four pillars (ACE/ARB/ARNI + BB + MRA + SGLT2i) - introduce early, titrate to max tolerated
  • HFmrEF: Consider as extension of HFrEF - treat co-morbidities, add SGLT2i if diabetic/CKD
  • HFpEF: Control HR (target 70bpm), BP (per HTN guidelines), diabetes, anaemia (inc. iron studies); titrate diuretics to symptoms; consider SGLT2i if diabetic/CKD
  • All phenotypes: Monitor U&Es after medication changes, provide self-management education
  • Refer to specialist services if complex, unstable, or inadequate response to optimised therapy
